ARTIST STATEMENT

by

Benedicta Opoku-Mensah

I draw upon the essence of transformation, resilience, and the multidimensional aspect of human existence by using a variety of materials, each with its own distinct features and underlying significance.

My art becomes a reflection of the ever-changing human experience, and I hope to shed light on the complexities of our shared humanity.
Originating from Ghana, I have found myself immersed in a new and inspiring creative landscape abroad.
This geographical relocation has provided me with the opportunity to expand my artistic vocabulary.
It is amid this new environment that I have unearthed the beginning of a new chapter in my artistic journey.

In my artistic practice, I go on dynamic research of diverse materials and mediums, carefully selecting those that possess the intrinsic ability to best communicate my conceptual ideas.
The essence of my creative process is the diversity of expression, as I deal with metals, leather, wood, yarns, cords, fabric, and other materials.
My work serves as a potent vessel through which I engage with and dissect complex socio-political issues, as well as the profound concepts of self and identity and the beauty of cultural diversity.

Through my art, I strive to challenge preconceived notions of identity and its status quo. I create a space to invite the viewer to embark on reflective journeys of self-discovery and engage in the dialogue of change, growth, and self-realization.
